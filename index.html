BCNP

Bitcoin Communication Network Protocol

A post-IP data network stack rooted in Bitcoin and Lightning. BCNP replaces TCP/IP with decentralized address assignment and direct Ethernet framing between peers.

What is BCNP?

BCNP revives the spirit of legacy protocols like ISO CLNP originally used alongside TCP/IP and still employed in aviation and academic settings. It utilizes NSAP style addresses - up to 20 bytes long - for flexible, global identifiers.

With variable-length addressing and no hard limits, BCNP is ideal for emerging domains like IoT, AI, and sensitive industries; where scale, speed, and determinism matter most.

Why Now?

IPv4 to IPv6 transition stumbled due to legacy inertia and deployment complexity. BCNP offers a clean-slate alternative: decentralized, address-collision resistant, and privacy-first.
