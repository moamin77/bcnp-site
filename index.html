BCNP

Bitcoin Communication Network Protocol

A post-IP data network stack rooted in Bitcoin and Lightning. BCNP replaces TCP/IP with decentralized address assignment and direct Ethernet framing between peers.

What is BCNP?

BCNP revives the spirit of legacy protocols like ISO CLNP originally used alongside TCP/IP and still employed in aviation and academic settings. It utilizes NSAP style addresses - up to 20 bytes long - for flexible, global identifiers.

With variable-length addressing and no hard limits, BCNP is ideal for emerging domains like IoT, AI, and sensitive industries; where scale, speed, and determinism matter most.

Why Now?

IPv4 to IPv6 transition stumbled due to legacy inertia and deployment complexity. BCNP offers a clean-slate alternative: decentralized, address-collision resistant, and privacy-first.

Address issuance is performed via Bitcoin or Lightning: a blockchainâ€‘enabled mechanism ensures security, sovereignty, and neutrality, free from centralized control.

Vision

BCNP aims to evolve into a global, open communication layer that eventually replaces IP-based networking for systems that demand decentralization, autonomy, and security. While inspired by Bitcoin and Lightning, BCNP is designed to serve any ecosystem that values freedom from legacy infrastructure.

We believe in a hybrid future: BCNP will remain open and extensible, but early adoption may be driven by organizations needing highly secure, isolated, and deterministic environments. From IoT and edge AI, to zero-trust zones, R&D networks, or ultra-sensitive corporate domains; BCNP offers an IP-free alternative that scales.

Whether you're building for global freedom or private sovereignty, BCNP provides the tools to move beyond the bottlenecks of TCP/IP.
