BCNP

Bitcoin Communication Network Protocol

A post-IP data network architecture rooted in the principles of Bitcoin and Lightning. BCNP breaks free from IP, DNS, and NAT, replacing them with clean, prefix-based identity and local-first communication.

What is BCNP?

BCNP is a protocol that enables peer communication without relying on the legacy IP stack. Each device constructs its address using a gateway-assigned prefix and its MAC address, resulting in 20-byte NSAP-style identifiers. No IP. No ports. No DNS.

Why Now?

TCP/IP was never built for decentralization, privacy, or sovereignty. In the age of Bitcoin, these are no longer optional ‚Äî they are foundational. BCNP rethinks communication at the data layer, the same way Bitcoin rethought money.

How it Works

Gateways assign prefixes to clients via an ES-IS-like Hello protocol. Clients form their BCNP address by combining the prefix with their MAC address. Frames are sent directly over Ethernet or encapsulated over tunnels across networks ‚Äî all without relying on IP.

Global Tunnel Demo

Two closed BCNP networks ‚Äî one in the U.S., one in Asia ‚Äî exchange data over a tunnel that spans the world. No IP addresses involved. Just raw frames wrapped, routed, and delivered using BCNP.
