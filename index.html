BCNP

Bitcoin Communication Network Protocol

What is BCNP?

BCNP is a working prototype of a post-IP data networking layer. Modeled after ISO CLNP and inspired by Bitcoin’s architecture, it challenges legacy assumptions of DNS, NAT, and centralized routing.

Why Now?

TCP/IP was never designed with privacy, sovereignty, or decentralization in mind. In a world reshaped by Bitcoin and Lightning, the foundation of how devices connect needs a similar rethink.

How it Works

Devices construct their addresses from gateway-assigned prefixes and their MAC address, forming 20-byte NSAP-style IDs. BCNP eliminates IP addressing and replaces it with clean, prefix-based, link-layer communication.
