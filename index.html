BCNP

Bitcoin Communication Network Protocol

A post-IP data network architecture rooted in the principles of Bitcoin and Lightning. BCNP breaks free from IP, DNS, and NAT, replacing them with clean, prefix-based identity and local-first communication.

What is BCNP?

BCNP is a protocol that enables peer communication without relying on the legacy IP stack. Each device constructs its address using a gateway-assigned prefix and its MAC address, resulting in 20-byte NSAP-style identifiers. No IP. No ports. No DNS.

Why Now?

TCP/IP was never built for decentralization, privacy, or sovereignty. In the age of Bitcoin, these are no longer optional – they are foundational. BCNP rethinks communication at the data layer, the same way Bitcoin rethought money.

How it Works

Gateways assign prefixes to clients via an ES-IS-like hello protocol. Clients form their BCNP address by combining the prefix with their MAC address. Frames are sent directly over Ethernet or encapsulated over tunnels across networks – all without relying on IP.

Global Tunnel Demo

Two closed BCNP networks – one in San Francisco, one in Tokyo – exchange encapsulated frames over a tunnel that spans the globe. This isn't packet routing. This is raw data movement – deterministic and post-IP.
